Gloss (author-provided description)

Given a complete lattice of candidate distinguishability structures on a state space, a set of structures that comply with an observation specification (closed under taking suprema), and a readability relation that is preserved toward finer structures (whatever a coarser structure can read, every finer structure below it can also read), there is a single greatest compliant structure, and anything readable at that greatest structure is readable under every compliant structure. The greatest compliant structure is fixed by the specification alone.

**Cites** `WMSpec.Grammar.ObservationGrammar.forcing_principle` — the corpus theorem this result restates and discharges.

**Original contribution.** The corpus lemma proves the greatest-element existence plus readability transfer; the thesis statement names this the root forcing principle at lattice generality and positions it as the single abstraction that the measurable, kernel-level, relational, and metric results below all specialize.
