An encoder qualifies as a world model exactly when the partition it induces (its kernel) conforms to the targets and dynamics. Only the partition matters; nothing else about the encoder does.

**Cites** `TLT.NonIdentifiability.isWorldModel_iff_ker_isConforming` — the corpus theorem this result restates and discharges.

**Original contribution.** The corpus lemma is the iff characterization; the thesis frames it as the statement that world-model-hood is a property of the induced partition alone, the bridge that lets the learning-theoretic legs treat encoders purely by the partitions they induce.
