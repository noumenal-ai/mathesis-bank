There is a concrete two-bit causal model where switching which family of measurements is available (from the observational family to a richer response-type family, without adding any new data) shrinks the residual spread of an interventional quantity from one-half to zero. This shows the companion invariance results are not empty: the spread stays fixed when you merely re-weight the same information, but genuinely moves when you change the structure of what can be distinguished.

**Cites** `WMSpec.Transform.support_moves_width` — the corpus theorem this result restates and discharges.

**Original contribution.** The corpus lemma supplies the single separating example; the thesis frames it as the necessary companion that makes the two invariance theorems non-vacuous, contrasting a quantity that is fixed along the re-weighting axis with one that changes along the structural axis, at the same model.
