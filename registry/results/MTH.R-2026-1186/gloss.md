Away from resonance, two normalized qubit states become indistinguishable under repeated position-expectation measurement exactly when one is obtained from the other by a phase rotation, or by a conjugation-swap followed by a phase rotation. These physical symmetry orbits emerge from a single observable's dynamics even though no symmetry structure was supplied.
