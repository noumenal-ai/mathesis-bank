Over an arbitrary measurable state space with an arbitrary measure-valued family of dynamics, the bisimilarity relation for a target family is itself a valid spec-respecting equivalence, is coarser than every other such equivalence, and every encoder that satisfies the specification has a kernel at least as fine as it. No countability, finiteness, or topology is assumed.

**Cites** `WMSpec.General.forcing_theoremM` — the corpus theorem this result restates and discharges.

**Original contribution.** The corpus lemma is the measurable-space forcing theorem; the thesis restates it at the same exact type but frames it as the measurable-space instance of the one forcing principle, making explicit that the coarsest spec-respecting equivalence exists with no regularity assumptions.
