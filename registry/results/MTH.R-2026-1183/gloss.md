The residual spread of any metric-valued quantity across the states you cannot tell apart is the same for any two measurement families that collapse the state space the same way. How much you remain uncertain about a function of the state is fixed by the collapsed structure alone, not by the choice of measurements.

**Cites** `WMSpec.Transform.fiberWidth_support_invariant` — the corpus theorem this result restates and discharges.

**Original contribution.** Where the cited lemma states width equality under the bisimulation hypothesis, the thesis lifts it to a general functional F valued in any pseudo-metric space and reads it as: the measurable spread of an unknown quantity is a functional of the collapsed structure alone, invariant under re-encoding the measurements.
